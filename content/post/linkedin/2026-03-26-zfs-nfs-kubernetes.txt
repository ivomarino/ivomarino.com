Kubernetes storage is broken.

Your options:
- Ephemeral storage: lose data on pod crash
- Cloud storage (EBS, GCP Persistent Disks): $500+/month, vendor lock-in, no snapshots
- DIY NFS: fragile, no recovery, no backups

We chose the fourth option: a 2vCPU, 4GB RAM FreeBSD VM running ZFS + NFS in the same VPC as our K8s cluster.

Here's what changed:

**Point-in-time recovery.** Hourly snapshots cost almost nothing in storage space (copy-on-write). Pod crashes? Data's intact on NFS. App bug corrupts data? Rollback in 5 minutes. Accidental delete? Clone from yesterday's snapshot.

**True multi-cluster storage.** Mount the same NFS export from staging + production simultaneously. Share storage across cluster boundaries without vendor APIs.

**Automated backups.** zfs-autobackup replicates incremental snapshots nightly to offsite storage. First sync is full, then only changed blocks transfer. Recovery time: minutes.

**Real numbers:**
- VM cost: ~$30-40/month
- Offsite storage: ~$50/month
- Total: ~$80-90/month

vs $500+/month for managed solutions.

The whole setup is open-source: FreeBSD, ZFS, NFS, zfs-autobackup, CARP (for HA).

No vendor lock-in. No per-gigabyte fees. No hoping someone else's backup works.

Just reliable storage that actually lets you recover data.
